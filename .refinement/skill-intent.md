# Skill INTENT Analysis: aim-website-refresh

## Q1: Who is this for?

Local antique shoppers, collectors, and casual browsers in Moore, Oklahoma and the surrounding OKC metro area. Demographics skew older (40-70+) but include younger collectors and weekend browsers of all ages. Technical sophistication is average to low -- these are people who find the shop through Facebook, word of mouth, or drive-by visibility. Emotional state on arrival: curiosity, weekend leisure mode, treasure-hunting anticipation. They are not in a hurry and not comparison-shopping against e-commerce; they are looking for a reason to visit a physical place.

## Q2: What must this communicate?

**Warmth and discovery.** The primary signal is "this is a place worth visiting" -- a curated marketplace that feels welcoming, not intimidating. Secondary signals: community (50+ vendors, active social presence), legitimacy (real address, real hours, real photos), and the feeling of stumbling onto something special. The site should communicate that AIM is a destination, not just a store. Trust through authenticity (real photos of real inventory), not through corporate polish.

## Q3: What is the context?

Antique malls in Oklahoma operate in a low-competition web space -- most have Facebook pages at best, or dated WordPress sites with stock photos. The competitive bar is extremely low. Industry norms lean rustic-kitsch (wood plank textures, barn imagery, distressed fonts). The scope explicitly rejects this: "Not rustic-kitsch. Curated marketplace aesthetic -- clean enough to be modern, warm enough to feel like discovery." The brand identity constraint is text-based branding ("Antiques in Moore" in serif type) with warm earth tones. Stitch design reference (project 12648737340326082222) establishes Libre Caslon Text headlines, Source Sans 3 body, earth tones, tonal spot color, round-8 corners.

## Q4: What is the one thing someone should remember?

The warm, curated visual feel -- "the antique mall with the good website." In a market where competitors have no web presence or have dated pages, a clean, warm, photo-rich site IS the differentiator. The color palette (aged wood, warm metals, Oklahoma red earth) and real gallery photos of actual inventory create a sensory impression that separates AIM from every other antique mall in the state.

## Q5: What does the builder know that an AI would not default to?

- The audience is primarily Facebook-driven. Facebook links and social proof (3K followers, 94% recommend) are primary trust signals, not testimonials or press logos.
- The store is 8,000 sq ft with 50+ vendors -- this is a substantial operation, not a hobby booth. The site needs to communicate scale without feeling corporate.
- Hours are unusual: Friday 12-5, Saturday-Sunday 9-5. Not typical retail hours. The site must make hours immediately findable to prevent wasted trips.
- The existing brand identity is text-based (no graphic logo file exists). "Antiques in Moore" in serif type IS the logo.
- The aesthetic direction references Oklahoma red earth specifically -- not generic "brown" or "rust." The landscape reference point is the red soil visible on every dirt road in the region.
- The Stitch design reference (project 12648737340326082222) has already been approved: Libre Caslon Text for headlines, Source Sans 3 for body, earth tones, tonal spot color variant, round-8 corners.
