# Skill Direction Selection: aim-website-refresh

## Step 1: Landscape Loaded

All 7 named directions reviewed: Training Default, Dark Minimal, Warm Editorial, Precision Instrument, Energetic Saturated, Soft Organic, Neobrutalist. Direction Comparison Reference table consulted.

## Step 2: Dimensional Mapping from INTENT

### Axis 1: Formality -> Casual-to-mid
INTENT Q1 identifies leisure-mode visitors (weekend browsers, treasure hunters) and Q2 emphasizes warmth and discovery over authority. The site should feel "authored by a person" -- approachable, curated, personal. Not casual-playful (this is a real business), but not corporate (antique malls are inherently personal spaces).

### Axis 2: Density -> Spacious-editorial
INTENT Q1 describes users in leisure mode who are not scanning for data efficiency. Q2 calls for a "destination" feeling with room for photos to breathe. The site has minimal content (2 pages, a few sections) and the goal is atmosphere, not information density. Generous margins and deliberate pacing serve this project.

### Axis 3: Saturation -> Low-to-mid
INTENT Q4 specifies the color palette: "aged wood, warm metals, Oklahoma red earth." These are earth tones -- muted, warm, natural. Not vivid-expressive (no neon or saturated primaries) but not purely neutral either. The red earth tone functions as a warm accent, not a muted gray. One warm accent color plus a warm neutral scale with potential for a secondary accent (warm metal / brass tone).

### Axis 4: Warmth -> Warm-organic
INTENT Q2 explicitly calls for warmth. Q3 explicitly rejects the cool/neutral alternative ("not rustic-kitsch" but warm). Q5 references Oklahoma red earth, aged wood, warm metals -- all natural, warm material references. Cream/warm white surfaces, rounded corners, soft shadows, natural color references are all directly indicated.

### Axis 5: Ornament -> Low-to-mid
INTENT Q3 says "clean enough to be modern, warm enough to feel like discovery." This is not minimal-functional (that reads as cold/institutional for an antique mall) but not decorative-expressive (no wood plank textures or vintage clip art). Subtle warmth through typography and spacing, occasional photographic texture, but restrained decoration. The photos ARE the ornament.

### Axis 6: Motion -> Mid-range
INTENT Q1 describes leisure-mode browsing where gentle scroll reveals and subtle transitions enhance the "discovery" feeling. Not restrained-subtle (that feels clinical) but not expressive-dynamic (that is distracting for this audience). Gentle easing, scroll-triggered reveals, smooth page transitions serve the pacing.

## Step 3: Direction Selection

**Selected Direction: Warm Editorial**

**Dimensional Profile Match:**

| Axis | INTENT Requirement | Warm Editorial Profile | Match |
|------|-------------------|----------------------|-------|
| Formality | Casual-to-mid | Casual-to-mid | Exact |
| Density | Spacious-editorial | Spacious-editorial | Exact |
| Saturation | Low-to-mid | Low-to-mid | Exact |
| Warmth | Warm-organic | Warm-organic | Exact |
| Ornament | Low-to-mid | Low-to-mid | Exact |
| Motion | Mid-range | Mid-range | Exact |

Warm Editorial is an exact match on all 6 axes. No hybrid needed.

**INTENT Justification:**
- INTENT Q2 identifies warmth, discovery, and approachability as the primary communication needs -- these are the defining characteristics of Warm Editorial ("the feel of a well-designed magazine spread or indie publication, warmth through typography choices, organic spacing rhythm").
- INTENT Q4 identifies the warm earth-tone color palette as the memorable differentiator -- Warm Editorial's characteristic markers include "accent colors drawn from warm spectrum (terracotta, olive, amber, dusty rose)" which directly maps to the Oklahoma red earth, aged wood, and warm metal palette.
- INTENT Q3 explicitly matches Warm Editorial's example project types: "artisan craft shop" and "boutique" contexts where "humanity and authorship matter."

No other direction fits:
- Training Default: lacks the deliberate warmth and personality (Q2, Q4)
- Dark Minimal: wrong warmth pole entirely, wrong audience context (Q1)
- Precision Instrument: wrong on every axis for a leisure antique mall
- Energetic Saturated: wrong formality and saturation for this audience (Q1)
- Soft Organic: close on warmth but too pastel/illustration-focused; AIM's strength is real photography, not illustration
- Neobrutalist: wrong formality, wrong warmth, wrong audience entirely
